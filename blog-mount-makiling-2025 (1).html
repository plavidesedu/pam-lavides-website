Adventure · Mount Makiling, Laguna · Part 1

Make It Makiling 2025!

That was our chant by the end of the day — though at the start, none of us had any idea what we'd actually signed up for. Here's how it all went down.

How I Got Talked Into This

I've been hiking mountains since 2018, so Makiling was roughly my fifth. But my last real climb was Mount Daraitan back in 2023, which meant a whole year had gone by without a single trail under my boots. Then, out of nowhere, my niece Molyn messaged me on Messenger: did I want to hike Mount Makiling? Her cousin Joie (also my niece — Filipino families, you know how it is) had invited her, and Molyn figured she'd rope me in too. I said yes before I'd even finished reading the message.

Assembling the Squad

On the day itself, my sister Ate Mika — hiking with me for the very first time — drove us to UPLB to meet Joie and Molyn. That's where the surprises started stacking up: Molyn's dad, Kuya Jason (my cousin, better known as Moré), showed up too, along with Joie's husband Christian, his brother Kirk, and Kirk's girlfriend Anne. What was supposed to be a small group turned into a full squad. Honestly? The more, the merrier.

Before we could take a single step, the UP volunteers ran us through a quick orientation — the usual "leave no trash, only footprints" reminders and basic safety pointers. Then it was straight to registration: names down, ₱60 paid, and we were off.

Into the Forest

The trail didn't ease us in — it was uphill from the very first step, a wide path swallowed up by tall trees on both sides. A few minutes in and it already felt endless. But the air was crisp and cool, which made the climb almost forgiving, and the only soundtrack was birdsong, crickets, and everyone's chatter and laughter echoing through the trees. We clocked in at 6:21 AM.

By 7:57 AM, our legs were starting to complain — one long uphill grind will do that — but spirits stayed high. Nobody was ready to call it quits, and the promise of the Mudspring kept us moving.

The Boiling Mudspring

We rolled up to the Mudspring at 8:12 AM and just stood there for a second — steam was rising straight off the water, like it was actually boiling. We took our time exploring, snapping photos, and soaking in how strange and cool the whole thing looked, right there along the trail, before pushing on.

The next stretch was another long haul, and at some point the "should we just turn back" jokes started flying. What kept us going was Kuya Jason and Molyn's promise of a store somewhere up ahead. Kirk, for his part, was holding out hope for halo-halo. Bold strategy on a mountain trail, but we respected it.

Agila Base (a.k.a. the Snack Stop)

At 9:10 AM, we finally reached Agila Base — the place Kuya Jason had been hyping up the entire hike without ever explaining what it actually was. Turns out it's where the paved road ends, and yes, there were stores. And yes, they had halo-halo. It's also where the base volunteers are stationed, and where you register a second time — this time for the real climb, up to the summit known as Peak 2.

Surprise: the actual trail hadn't even started yet.

The Real Climb Begins

Past Agila Base, the pavement disappears completely — from here on, it's pure forest trail, straight uphill. We started from Station 11, with Peak 2 waiting all the way up at Station 30. (For reference: everything we'd just walked was the road from Station 10 heading down — basically the warm-up.)

So there we were, standing at the true starting line, still fueled up on energy, snacks, and a little too much confidence. We were ready to take on Mount Makiling for real.
