Hiking · 2025

Mt. Makiling — 2025

Trip

Make it Makiling 2025! That was our catchphrase when we finally reached the peak of Mount Makiling. But how did this hike actually begin? Haha!

I've hiked several mountains since 2018 — Mount Makiling is probably my fifth. My last hike was in 2023 at Mount Daraitan, so I went a whole year without any climbs. Then one day, my niece Molyn sent me a message on Messenger, asking if I wanted to hike Mount Makiling. Apparently, her cousin Joie (who's also my niece) invited her, and Molyn thought of inviting me, too. I got excited right away and said yes!

Fast forward to the hike day — my sister, Ate Mika (her first time hiking with me), drove us to UPLB where we met up with Joie and Molyn. To my surprise, Molyn's dad, Kuya Jason (my cousin, a.k.a. Moré), also came along! He was with Joie, her husband Christian, his brother Kirk, and Kirk's girlfriend Anne. Turns out, everyone was joining the hike. So fun! The more, the merrier, right?

When we all gathered, the UP volunteers gave us a quick orientation — basic reminders like "leave no trash, only footprints," and a few safety guidelines. Then we went straight to registration, wrote down our names, paid the ₱60 fee, and off we went.

As we started walking, we were all pumped. The road was uphill right from the beginning — wide paths surrounded by tall trees. After several minutes, it felt like the road just never ended! But the air was crisp and cold, so it was refreshing to walk. You could hear nothing but the chirping of birds, the hum of crickets, and the laughter and chatter of fellow hikers.

We started the trek around 6:21 AM. By 7:57 AM, we were heading toward the Mudspring. We were already feeling a bit tired and our legs were sore — it was just one long, uphill stretch! But spirits were still high, and we were excited for the adventure.

We reached the Mudspring at around 8:12 AM and were amazed to see smoke rising from the water — it was literally boiling! We all took some time to explore, take photos, and just take in the moment. The Mudspring is right along the Makiling trail, and after a bit of sightseeing, we continued on our trek.

It was another long walk from there. We were already joking about turning back, but Kuya Jason and Molyn promised there'd be a store up ahead — which kept us motivated. Kirk was even hoping they'd have halo-halo, LOL!

At 9:10 AM, we finally arrived at the Agila Base — finally! That's what Kuya Jason kept mentioning, but I had no idea what it was until we got there. It turned out to be the end of the paved road, and yes, there were stores… and they had halo-halo! It was also where the base volunteers were stationed, and where we had to register again — this time, for the actual climb to the summit, known as Peak 2. Surprise! The real trail was just beginning.

From there, there were no more roads — only a forest trail heading uphill. We started from Station 11 at Agila Base, and Peak 2 was all the way up at Station 30. (For reference, the long road we walked earlier was from Station 10 going down.) So this was it — we were finally starting the actual trail hike. Everyone was up for the challenge. We still had energy and strength, and we were ready to conquer Mount Makiling!
